Jeopardy!'s Ken Jennings claps back at angry fan who accuses host of adding too much 'commentary'

KEN Jennings got into it with a Jeopardy! fan who had accused him of "too much commentary" while hosting.

The TV personality joked he was "looking into this now."

The Twitter user wrote: "Listen Ken Jennings just read the answers stop with the commentary #Jeopardy #a**wagon."

Ken, 48, quote-tweeted them and shaded: "Looking into this now."

Another user replied: "I didn't realize you also host A**wagon!" to which Ken responded once more since the add-on was too funny to ignore.

"It really hosts itself," Ken added.

Jeopardy! fans came to Ken's defense in the reply section and agreed they like his ability to ad-lib with the contestants and make them feel at ease.

Others just had a laugh over his unabashed tendency to come for his critics.

One wrote: "Ken, that’s nonsense. You do just the right amount of commentary, like a pro."

"Do not stop the commentary- you add a lot to the show!" wrote another.

"Interesting," a third simply wrote on the particularly spicy back-and-forth exchange.

"Please find a way to make 'a**wagon' a Jeopardy! clue," begged a fifth.

A sixth agreed: "Meh… I mean if you're just gonna read the answers might as well get a ChatGPT [bot] to do it."

"I would've said Watson but that would've been a low blow," they added, referencing how the 74-time winner once lost to an IBM computer.

KEN'S COMMENTS

The contestant-turned-host's first official stint as presenter began in September.

He has admittedly been clocked by some critics for ad-libbing in a way they've accused could affect gameplay.

Particularly, Ken was called out when 21-day winner Cris Pannullo was winning big this fall.

During one game well into Cris' $748K streak, Ken called the player's impressive ability to rack up massive amounts of money by finding Daily Doubles a "spidey sense."

"Well, you've found them all again but you're one for two,” Ken also told the champ later in that game - seeming to imply he should wager high to increase his lead.

During Mattea Roach's 23-game run last season, Ken also started opening the show with monologues about Mattea in front of the new players.

In one, he noted that they were Canadian, before listing other people, places, and things from the country.

One fan slammed: "It's really annoying because Ken Jennings more than anyone should know his talking, his favoritism, all of it is unfair. WTF?"

Ken was also accused of egging on Amy Schneider during her 27th win of an eventual 40.

Ken said before one Final Jeopardy: "I bet you [Amy] would like to have $1,401 more right now because this is not a runaway."

Pointing out exactly how much she should wager seemed a bit much, and in the past months, Ken has done less ad-libbing in terms of gameplay.

'NOPE'

Over the summer, Ken and actress Mayim Bialik, 47, were announced as the permanent replacements for the late longtime host, Alex Trebek.

Alex tragically passed away from pancreatic cancer at age 80 in 2020, leaving huge shoes to fill, and it took two drama-filled years to fill them.

The game show's first host pick, Mike Richards, who was coincidentally also its executive producer at the time, was ousted after a week of episodes when he was discovered to have made sexist comments.

Ken's first hosting stint is coming to an end as Mayim is switching in starting mid-February.

When Mayim gets her due, she'll be starting with a teen tournament which will then lead to more regular episodes.

Fans will be getting more of Ken as he will be hosting the new Jeopardy! Masters spinoff, which is scheduled to air during the spring in primetime.

Last month a Twitter user implied Ken was actually better than the late Alex.
