A ROOSEVELT dime could potentially be worth hundreds of dollars – and you’ll want to look out for a couple of things. 

Since 1946, the Roosevelt dime has been struck by the US Mint. 

However, it can be tough to find something valuable in that particular series mainly due to there not being many low-mintage coins. 

But there’s something special about one date, as pointed out by a coin expert who goes by BlueRidgeSilverHound, or Shaun, on TikTok. 

He has more than 250,000 subscribers on YouTube and often shares content informing his followers about what coins are valuable.

Here’s what Shaun revealed about a rare Roosevelt dime in a recent video and what you need to look out for.

What is the dime and how to spot it? 

In 1982, the Philadelphia mint accidentally produced a small number of dimes without a letter mark.

The coin was supposed to bear a “P” mark to indicate where it was produced.

However, an employee created an error by failing to add it to the obverse of the die.

As a result, roughly 8,000 to 10,000 were found in Sandusky, Ohio. 

There were also more dimes without mint marks found in Pittsburgh and Boston.

On the obverse, you’ll want to look out for a 1982 date.

If there is no letter mark above it, then you have something of value.

Also, there are two varieties to look out for, according to Shaun.

  • Weak strike
  • Strong strike

The weak strike will show the date fading away, while the stronger will be printed clearer.

How much are they worth? 

But regardless, both varieties are valuable.

How much they are worth will depend on grade and condition.

“They average from $50 to $250,” Shaun said of the 1982 dimes that don’t have mintmarks.

But they could even be worth more in high grades.

For example, a 1982 dime that was graded in Mint State (MS) 67 sold for $720 in an October auction, according to Professional Coin Grading Services.

Keep in mind that MS means that the coin is uncirculated, meaning these grades do not contain traces of wear.

Some recent circulated examples have sold for $202.49, $202 and $123.52 online. 

Other valuable Roosevelt dimes

Unfortunately, your Roosevelt dimes won't likely be worth a whole lot unless you have an error piece.

A Roosevelt dime with cud error recently sold for a total of $119.26 on eBay after 15 bids.

Also, a 2002 error dime recently sold for $200.

To see if your spare change is worth anything, you can check eBay by searching the full name, selecting the "sold" listing and then toggling the search to "highest value”.
