I wanted extra money so got a job that people judge me for - but I earn an extra 1k a month &amp; can

A WOMAN has opened up about how she makes an extra £1k in cash a month by doing a job that some people judge her for.

Jessie Tyron revealed that she wanted to up her earnings and so decided to make the decision to sell pictures of her feet online to strangers. 

Since starting the online business four months ago, Jessie has seen the financial rewards – earning £1000 a month from the side hustle. 

Jessie also shared how or her best individual day, she took home over £200 after charging £1 per picture.

The job can be done anywhere using just a phone, but Jessie confessed that it’s not for everyone and many believe it to be extremely controversial.” 

The TikTok user, who can be found at her profile @jessietyron, explained: “I sell feet content, which is basically feet pictures, videos, video calls.”

In another video, she said: “So I started doing this about four months ago and the most I’ve made in one month is over £1k.”

Jessie then revealed the ways in which she’d maximised her earnings, having picked up some top tips along the way including making a menu of everything she offers in content. 

But it didn’t take long for her to detail the downsides of the job. 

Jessie explained: “By the way, you can get a lot of hate for doing this, so I’m just putting it out there.

“I’m quite a strong person so I can deal with it, I mean, kind of strong, but you do get a lot of hate.

“And if people you know find out, they may not be supportive of it, but luckily for me, they are.”

Jessie was also keen to clear up any rumours, as she said: “I do not sell anything nudity, so it’s only feet, sometimes a little bit of my legs.

“But strictly feet, I do not show my face in my content and I do not talk in content, not on video calls or anything like that.” 

Jessie is far from the only person to turn to this type of side hustle to help out in the cost of living crisis. 

Last year, TikTok user Cat mama told her 27.3k followers that her similar business had become so lucrative that she now sold foot photos full time. 

The then 20-year-old social media user showed how her finances had grown due to her side hustle. 

In one screenshot she revealed she raked in £647 and £500 for selling the snaps.
