Rare green-winged orchid flourishes during lockdown putting half a golf course out of bounds

A RARE orchid has flourished during lockdown, putting half a golf course out of bounds.

Players desperate to tee off after months away were frustrated to find nine holes shut thanks to the green-winged orchid.

The flower prospered as grass was not cut on parts of the Ashton Court course in Bristol.

A ranger said: “The golfers aren’t particularly pleased about it, but we have hundreds of orchids that have sprouted up on the fairway.

"The flowers have gone now, but we are keen to make sure the seeds mature so we get more next year.”

Golfer Phil Bloxham, 58, believes closure was unnecessary, and the flowers could have been roped off.

He said: “Most people are sensible about the importance of wild flowers and would give them a wide berth.”

The green winged orchid, which is regularly pollinated by bumblebees, is classified as 'near threatened' by government conservationists.

A Bristol City Council spokesperson said: “Following the closure of golf facilities during the Covid-19 lockdown, maintenance was reduced.

"As a result, orchids, including the rare green winged orchid, have been able to grow on parts of the course.

“In order to protect these flowers, we have taken the decision to reduce the number of holes available to play from 18 to nine.

"This will both allow the plants to seed and enhance the environment for pollinators.

“We would ask the public to be patient and respectful of our staff as they work to make as many services available as possible in a safe way during unprecedented circumstances.’’
