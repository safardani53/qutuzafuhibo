7 Best Car Cleaning Sponges | The Sun UK

WASHING your car in a drive-through machine might be cheap and low-effort, but you’ll get a much better clean if you do it by hand yourself – plus you’ll get a bonus workout too.

We've rounded up the best cleaning sponges for your car right now.

The experts recommend washing your car every two weeks to keep it free from the damage caused by dirt, bird droppings, grease, acidic rain and salt water, but most of us don’t even manage half that unfortunately.

When you do (finally) decide to wash your car, make sure you choose a dull, cool day – water evaporates too quickly from a hot car body and will leave you with annoying streaks that are difficult to remove.

Respect your motor and use a quality car shampoo and a car sponge designed specially for the job.

Both will protect your paintwork and stop your car getting so-called swirl marks – superfine scratch marks caused by dust and poor washing technique.

Do not be tempted to use dishwashing liquid to wash your car – it might be great at getting your dishes sparkling clean but it isn’t going to do your motor any favours and in fact, dishwashing liquid and other household cleaners can even encourage rust by stripping your car’s paintwork of its protective wax.

Rinsing your cleaning sponge regularly will also help to prevent dreaded swirl marks – have a separate bucket of clean water to rinse your sponge off with as you go along.

Check out the RAC site for more cleaning tips and read on for our round-up of the best car-cleaning sponges to help keep your car sparkling clean at home.
