Bankrupt beloved retailer closes all stores as online company wins $21.5million auction - see if you

ALL Bed Bath & Beyond stores will shutter this summer, but the retailer’s new buyer will keep charge of key online assets from the company.

After a successful auction win, online retailer Overstock.com has purchased Bed Bath & Beyond’s intellectual property and mobile platform, court filings showed Thursday.

The total sale for the property from the bankrupt retail giant will take place for $21.5million.

However, the sale will still need to be approved at a hearing on Tuesday.

While Overstock will have access to Bed Bath & Beyond’s online platform, data and publicity rights, the stores are not included in the deal.

This means all the 350 remaining stores still have an upcoming closing date in June, marking the end of Bed Bath & Beyond’s reign in malls nationwide.

Read more about store closures

In a statement to The U.S. Sun, the company said: "We are closing select Bed Bath & Beyond and buybuy Baby stores across the country through the end of June."

A spokesperson confirmed the sale to The U.S. Sun on Thursday.

"Following completion of the court-approved auction process and in consultation with the lenders and official committee of unsecured creditors, Bed Bath & Beyond Inc. has selected a proposal from Overstock.com as the winning bidder," the spokesperson said.

The future of Bed Bath & Beyond’s buybuy Baby brand is still up in the air, however.

Most read in Money

The children’s brand has its auction set for next week, where investment firms Go Global Retail and Sixth Street Partners will likely present offers.

Go Global Retail, which owns children's apparel brand Janie and Jack, may keep some stores open if the chain is acquired through a deal.

Neil Saunders, a retail analyst and managing director at GlobalData Retail, said buybuy Baby “is the one part of the operation that will probably attract interest from buyers,” according to CNN.

BED BATH & BEYOND’S DESCENT

As the retail environment faced significant changes in the post-pandemic world, consumers turned more toward online shopping.

This, combined with a general lower demand for discretionary items due to high rates of inflation, created a perfect storm for brick and mortars like Bed Bath & Beyond to fall under.

Co-founder Warren Eisenberg said in an interview in January: "We missed the boat on the internet."

Once the chain originally went public in 1992 with over 1,550 stores, it found widespread success until a few years ago.

In 2019, the company reported its first decline in annual sales, and it’s been in a downward spiral ever since.

The company officially filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in April, and its future has remained unknown until now.

POTENTIAL SALES

While many shoppers have been saddened to hear the beloved home goods giant is closing its doors for good, there are some silver linings for customers.

Due to its closing sales, the store is offering massive discounts to customers while winding down operations.

The U.S. Sun has found multiple items with liquidation prices at 60 percent of the original price.

The company said on its Facebook page that it offers "deep discounts" for the store closing sale.

"New reductions are happening in store and new merchandise is arriving often," the post said.

The Facebook post advertised 30% to 50% off deals as the chain works to get rid of all remaining inventory. 

However, keep in mind, as the stores prepare for their final days, all sales will be final so you won’t be able to bring any items back for a refund.

MORE BANKRUPTCIES

As retailers struggle to make profits amid changing consumer trends, several other companies have filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy as well.

Party City and Tuesday Morning filed for bankruptcy and plan to close stores all across the country.

Meanwhile, David’s Bridal also is preparing to shutter its 300 stores as shoppers look for more affordable alternatives to wedding dress shopping.
