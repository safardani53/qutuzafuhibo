Premier League Christmas advent calendar - December 13: Pizzagate rocks the Premier League after Man

THEY called it ‘Pizzagate’ and ‘The Battle of the Buffet’ but, over a decade later, it’s still one of the most memorable – and funny – incidents in Premier League history.

In 2003/04, Arsenal’s team had remained unbeaten throughout the season and had played an angry goalless draw at Old Trafford where Martin Keown famously goaded Ruud van Nistelrooy after his missed penalty.

Tensions were still running high when the teams met again in Manchester in October 2004 with the Gunners aiming to make it 50 games unbeaten in the Premier League.

It was another feisty affair with tackles flying in all over the pitch and referee Mike Riley struggling to keep the peace.

And it didn’t end at the final whistle.

As United ran out 2-0 winners, the teams departed down the tunnel and as they reached the dressing rooms, it all kicked off, as Alex Ferguson explained. "The next thing I knew I had pizza all over me. We put food into the away dressing room after every game. Pizza, chicken. Most clubs do it. Arsenal's food was the best,” said the United boss. “They say it was Cesc Fabregas who threw the pizza at me but to this day, I have no idea who the culprit was."

But Arsenal’s Andy Cole knew. "This slice of pizza came flying over my head and hit Fergie straight in the mush. The slap echoed down the tunnel and everything stopped - the fighting, the yelling, everything. All eyes turned and all mouths gawped to see this pizza slip off that famous puce face and roll down his nice black suit.

"I thought Ferguson was going to explode but then he stormed off into the dressing room cursing and grunting, brushing the crumbs and stains off his collar. We all went back into the dressing room and fell about laughing. All I can say is that the culprit wasn't English or French, so that should narrow it down."
