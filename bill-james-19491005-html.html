George William James is an influential American baseball historian, writer, historian and statistician. Since 1977, James has written more than two dozen books devoted to baseball history and statistics. His approach, which he calls sabermetry by reference to the American Baseball Research Association, analyzes and studies baseball scientifically, often using statistical data, with the category Target determines why teams win and lose. In 2006, Time named him in Time 100 as one of the most influential people in the world. In 2003, James was hired as a senior baseball consultant for the Boston Red Sox.
