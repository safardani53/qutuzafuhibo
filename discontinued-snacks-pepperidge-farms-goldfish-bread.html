Five nostalgic treats you'll never see again - fans say they disappeared 'without even a warning'

EVERYONE has a treat they wish they could bring back to stores and enjoy just one more time.

Our childhood snacks hold a special place in our hearts, regardless of how long it has been since we've had them.

From fruit snacks to cupcakes and cookies, there was nothing better than getting home after school - or work - and enjoying your favorite treat.

Over the years many of these snacks have disappeared from grocery store shelves.

The U.S. Sun is taking a trip down memory lane and reminiscing over these five treats we miss dearly.

1. GOLDFISH SOFT BREAD

If you don't remember seeing these crust-free, goldfish-shaped slices of bread, you may remember the slogan of "A familiar smile â€¦ now in the bread aisle."

Everyone's favorite Goldfish crackers were transformed into white and honey whole wheat soft-baked bread, making it a hit with both parents and kids.

To celebrate Pepperidge Farm's 75th anniversary the company added brown sugar and cinnamon flavors to the lineup, according to Daily Meal.

2. CHOCOLATE AND COCONUT MILANOS

Another Pepperidge Farm classic is chocolate and coconut-flavored Milano cookies.

This iteration of the classic cookie added a layer of coconut cream on top of the chocolate layer.

On Twitter, one user wrote: "Me in Wegmans being told Pepperidge Farm discontinued the chocolate coconut cookies without even a warning," accompanied by a gif of a child repeatedly punching some packages of meat in a grocery store.

The company reached out to the shopper to let them know the cookies had been officially discontinued.

3. KEEBLER PIZZAIR PIZZA CHIPS

These chips were launched in 1991 and quickly became bestsellers.

The chips were made of pizza dough and coated in pizza seasonings.

Unfortunately, they were abruptly pulled from production in 2000.

Fans are still hoping to get these back on shelves, even going as far as to start petitions and Facebook groups to try and convince Keebler to change its mind.

4. GRAHAM CHEX

First hitting stores in 1993, Graham Chex was short-lived, lasting only a year in stores.

The cereal had graham-flavored Chex pieces and mini graham crackers.

A discontinued foods Twitter account (@Snack_Memories) posted a "rare box image" of the Graham Chex cereal, bringing back a slew of memories for many others.

"I started *making memories* shortly before this was discontinued, and I still remember it," one Twitter user said.

Another wrote: "I loved this cereal. This needs a remix."

"Oh man these were so good!" a third wrote.

5. BUTTERFINGER BB'S

You can't talk about iconic snacks without bringing up Butterfinger BB's.

This treat transformed Butterfinger bars into bite-sized little spheres that were perfect for snacking on the go.

They were first introduced in 1992 and quickly became popular through an ad campaign with the Simpsons.

Despite its popularity, the candy vanished from store shelves in 2006.

To this day people are still asking for a comeback.

"Still waiting on Butterfinger to bring back Butterfinger BB," one Twitter user said in a post last week.

"Last time I asked they unfollowed me, so might as well ask again."
