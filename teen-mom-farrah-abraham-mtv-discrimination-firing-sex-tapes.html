Teen Mom Farrah Abraham accuses MTV of 'discrimination' for firing her over sex tape as Briana DeJes

FARRAH Abraham slammed her former MTV home, accusing the network of discrimination and racism in light of her firing.

The Teen Mom was responding to a fan online, who wondered why co-star Briana DeJesus can appear on adult websites like Only Fans, or "post her naked body all over for (people) and keep her job, while Farrah was fired.

Farrah responded: "I'm 30 & I can do what I want & yes that is descrimination (sic) & racism."

She then took a swipe at the network, calling it an "awful company."

But another commenter pointed out that Briana didn't use her status on the MTV show to promote herself.

"Briana isn't calling her porn Teen Mom, (or) using the brand name" the user commented. "It's called conflict of interest, and a legit contract break.

Farrah, 30, was never a favorite among her castmates, and was often called out for her poor attitude and behavior.

But her entry into the adult film world seemed to have been the last straw.

It was no secret that Abraham was deemed difficult to work with during her tenure on Teen Mom. The young mom was called out by castmates and producers alike for her poor attitude and negative behavior. 

When producers discovered her extracurricular activities, they gave her an ultimatum: Teen mom or Adult Entertainment.

In a lengthy diatribe on Instagram, Farrah explained her decision:  "I chose FARRAH ABRAHAM. Ladies, becoming of age journey does not ever mean you have to succumb to a man's mediocrity, like the other weak teen moms have in every way."

It was revealed in Spetemberthat after nearly four years away, Farrah will be returning to the show that started it all for her. But it may not be a happy homecoming.

In a preview of the reunion, released exclusively to Entertainment Weekly, Farrah joined her former Teen Moms Amber Portwood and Maci Bookout for part one of MTV's Teen Mom: OG Reunion.

Premiering January 11 at 8 p.m. ET, the eight-episode series features favorites across the franchise, including Briana DeJesus, Jade Cline, and Leah Messer from Teen Mom 2.

As to what loyal enthusiasts can expect, Maci explains the reunion is more or less "all the casts living together" doing "physical fun activities, and we also had a life coach."

What the moms didn't know, was that they were going to be reunited with their old cast member, Farrah.

When asked about the experience filming with her again, Amber said: "We really tried to make her feel better...?"

Although Maci and Amber said that Farrah more or less encountered tensions with "pretty much everyone" on the set.
