Keith Richards (Guitarist) - Age, Birthday, Bio, Facts, Family, Net Worth, Height &amp; More

Keith Richards, commonly known as Keith Richard in the 1960s and 1970s, is an English musician, singer and songwriter. He is best known as the co-founder, guitarist, accompanist and co-composer of The Rolling Stones. Rolling Stone magazine called Richards the creator of the "greatest rock riff" on the guitar and ranked him fourth on the 2011 Top 100 Guitar List. The magazine lists fourteen songs Richards wrote. with the Rolling Stones lead singer Mick Jagger. Rolling Stone's list of "500 of the best songs of all time". Richards plays both the main guitar and rhythm, often on the same song; The Stones are commonly known for playing the main guitar between him and the band's other guitarists - Brian Jones, Mick Taylor or Ronnie Wood. In the recording studio, Richards played every single part of the guitar from time to time, including the songs "Paint It Black", "Ruby Tuesday", "Sympathy for the Devil" and "Gimme Shelter".
