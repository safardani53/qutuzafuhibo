MILLIONS of cash-strapped Americans are set to receive a direct payment worth up to $4,194 from next week.

Social Security benefits will start heading to claimants from December 14.

The check is being sent to seniors who have a birth date between the 1st and 10th of the month.

Social Security benefits will also be distributed on December 21 and 28.

Around 70million people in total receive Social Security.

Americans that decided to wait until age 70 before claiming Social Security can receive a payment worth up to $4,194.

The average monthly benefit is $1,657 – a $92 boost compared to last year.

For couples, it has jumped by $154 from $2,599 to $2,753.

But, the monthly amount for 2023 will increase from January in line with the cost-of-living adjustment.

The COLA was increased by 8.7 per cent earlier this year.

It was the biggest rise since 1981 when the COLA was 11.2 per cent.

It’s designed to keep up with inflation and is calculated on data from the Consumer Price Index for Urban Wage Earners and Clerical Workers – which measures the change in the cost of goods and services. 

It means that $144 will be added to the average benefit, providing an extra boost to citizens.

Kilolo Kijakazi, the Acting Commissioner of the Social Security Administration, said that the rise in benefits will give seniors “more peace of mind and breathing room”.

But, a poll conducted by the Motley Fool revealed that 55 per cent of Americans surveyed said the COLA rise wasn’t enough.

Some retirees receive less in Social Security checks based on where they live.

It affects seniors in Colorado, Connecticut, Kansas, Minnesota, Missouri, Montana, Nebraska, New Mexico, North Dakota, Rhode Island, Utah, Vermont, and West Virginia.

While many low-income claimants are exempt from state taxes, the tax can still put a dent in the checks of middle and upper-income earners.

American seniors that spend their retirement outside the US will still get the benefits, but those in nations such as Cuba, North Korea, Moldova, and Tajikistan are prohibited from earning the payments.
