Walmart has closed 23 stores with another scheduled to shut down this year - full list of locations

WALMART has shuttered 23 stores this year and has plans to shut down another.

The big-box retailer has been closing locations across the country all year.

Walmart recently announced that another store is on the chopping.

It is located in Norwalk, Connecticut, and it will be replaced by a Target.

The company chose not to renew the lease, leaving shoppers disappointed.

Missy Dye Radin lives near the store said: “The fact that I’m this close to Walmart, and I can just shoot over here and get what I need, is really convenient. It’s going to be a bummer.

read more on store closures

There are more than a half dozen Walmart locations in the surrounding areas, but for some shoppers like Radin, they aren't close enough.

“It’s quite a distance and frankly, it’s about convenience," she said.

“So I do think it’s a loss to this community because being able to quickly jump over to Walmart and get anything you need, knowing that you’re not paying a premium or a higher price, is valuable.” 

A spokesperson for Walmart confirmed to The U.S. Sun that it was a difficult decision to make.

"We are grateful to the customers who have given us the privilege of serving them at our Connecticut Avenue location," Walmart said.

"We look forward to serving them at our Main Avenue Supercenter and other stores in the surrounding communities and [online]."

The store is slated to close on November 3.

A SLEW OF CLOSURES

This announcement comes after Walmart shuttered 23 other locations this year.

In many of the cases, the retailer cited financial losses and underperforming stores as the reason for the closures.

In a statement, Walmart said its Chicago stores lose "tens of millions of dollars a year, and their annual losses nearly doubled in just the last five years," leading to several of them being shut down.
