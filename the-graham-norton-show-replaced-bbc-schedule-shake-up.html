The Graham Norton Show REPLACED as BBC One reveals major Friday night schedule shake-up

THE Graham Norton Show has been replaced in a major BBC One Friday night schedule shake-up.

The 60-year-old's popular chat show airs at 10.40pm on BBC One on Friday evenings.

Graham is joined by a range of celebrity guests and a music act each week in front of a studio audience.

However, it will be replaced by The Chris and Rosie Ramsey Show from May 12.

This is because Graham's show always takes a break over the summer, before returning later in the year.

Graham's last show of the current series aired at the end of February, followed by two compilation shows.

Since then, the late night Friday slot has been filled with movies, but from mid-May, the Ramsey's show will fill it.

Geordie comedian Chris, 36, and his wife Rosie first hosted their show in June last year.

The podcast superstars welcome celebrity couples for candid, comic chat - probing pet peeves, parenting problems and everything in between.

The first series included Martin and Shirley Kemp, Tom and Giovanna Fletcher and Martine McCutcheon and her husband Jack McManus.

The celebrity line-up for the new series is yet to be revealed.

Meanwhile Graham will be very busy in the coming weeks with his Virgin Radio Show, and his presenting duties on the Eurovision Song Contest 2023.

Ted Lasso star Hannah Waddingham will co-host the two semi-finals alongside Alesha Dixon and Ukrainian singer Julia Sanina on May 9 and 11.

Graham will then commentate on the grand final on Saturday, May 13.
