We review the BMW 2 Series Gran Tourer from price to economy and all its features

BMW has been to boot camp. This 7-seat MPV has the biggest load space of any Beemer.

And at £24,710 (£299 a month on finance) it’s almost half the price of the 7-seat X5.
But what is BMW doing making sensible MPVs in the first place?

They’re playing a clever game. They’re after young parents who like a badge. Parents with loads of kids/gear/pushchairs to lug around but who can’t afford 45 grand for an X5.

The compact 2 Series Gran Tourer fills that gap.

They get a premium car on their drive, yet can still ferry five kids in child seats in the back.

The second row seats tilt and slide (they flip down at the touch of a button in the boot) and even an adult can fit in the third row for short journeys.

But most impressive is the flat load space of 1,820 litres compared to 1,795 litres in the X5. It will easily swallow a single mattress.

For the record, the Gran Tourer is 20cm longer than the 5-seat Active Tourer launched last year and 5cm higher.

But up front everything else is the same. Head-up display, 6.5in monitor with split-screen satnav, DAB radio, dual-zone air con, handy storage and driver aids like park assist and adaptive cruise control.


I tested the £28,025 220i petrol in M Sport trim (18in alloys, styling kit, lower ride, sport seats) and the £32,540 220d xDrive diesel with four-wheel drive.

And don’t worry, they’ve still got it. They’ve still got BMW’s sporty driving DNA and enough poke (0-62mph in 7.8 seconds, top speed 139mph) to keep you entertained.

The kids should be happy too. There’s under-seat storage, fold-down tables and iPad holders.

So no more ‘Are we nearly there yet?’ - or they can sit in the boot.

Key Facts

  • Price: £28,025
  • Engine: 220i petrol (192hp) with 8-speed paddle shift
  • Economy: 47.9mpg
  • Road tax £130 (CO2 138g/km)
  • 0-62mph: 7.8 seconds
  • Top speed: 138mph
  • Out: June

Rivals: VW Touran, Ford Grand C-Max, Citroen C4 Grand Picasso, Renault
Grand Scenic, Vauxhall Zafira Tourer
Verdict: Room for kids and vroom for grown-ups.
